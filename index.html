The Place, Not the Name

At the edge of Mantle's industrial district stands a decommissioned heating spire, one of the old towers that once kept the city alive and was left to rust when the grid moved on. The lower floors hold a salvage and repair shop of no particular reputation. What the underworld calls "the Spire" is everything beneath it.

The Spire is not a syndicate. It holds no territory, sells no product on any street, and answers to no one. It is a workshop, and it does exactly one thing: work that nowhere else in Mantle can do, for people who can never be asked where they got it. Weapons rebuilt without records. Containers that pass under checkpoint eyes. Dust handled by someone who treats it less like a cargo and more like a language.

Few clients ever meet the engineer. Work is taken in and handed back by a man in old Vale armor who says little, counts everything, and is not paid to answer questions. Those who have been brought down into the workshop itself describe the same thing: a room that hums like the tower is still alive, and a host who is impossible to look away from. They mean that literally. Most of them don't go down a second time.

The Spire takes no sides, keeps no friends, and forgets nothing it has repaired. Everything has a price there. Nothing has a discount.

FLOOR B1 ·· RECOVERED RECORDS

The Tower That Stayed Warm

RECORD 01 ·· GRID ERA ·· [PARTIALLY RECOVERED]

When Atlas rose, it took the Kingdom's eyes with it. Mantle stayed below with a heating grid built for a living city and a budget written for a dying one. Dust flowed where the licenses were, and the licenses lived above the clouds. What the Kingdom called infrastructure, Mantle learned to call a privilege, rationed downward one cold winter at a time.

The Gridkeeper was one of the engineers who stayed when the maintenance contracts ended. The grid was failing and the Kingdom's answer was paperwork, so the Gridkeeper stopped filing it. Heating mains were Kingdom property, and repairing one without certification was sabotage under Atlas law. The lower city's answer to that was simple: the law had never kept anyone warm. A decommissioned heating spire at the edge of the industrial district became a workshop, because it sat on grid access the Kingdom had already written off. People brought heaters first. Then tools. Then weapons. The Gridkeeper fixed what came, priced what people could pay, and asked nothing, holding to a single conviction the way other people hold to faith: Dust and craft were the blood of the city, and no one above the clouds had the right to decide who deserved them.

The Kingdom never came for the tower. The Kingdom had stopped looking at Mantle altogether. That was the lesson the workshop was built on, and the lesson it never forgot.

RECORD 02 ·· THE QUIET WINTER ·· [GAP IN RECORD]

No one in the lower city can tell you when the Gridkeeper's story ends, because it doesn't end. One winter the tower simply went quiet. No body. No story. The district assumed the cold had finally collected its oldest debt, and the door stayed shut long enough that people stopped trying it.

What Mantle never learned was what the tower had taken in before it closed. A boy came down into the workshop that winter, or was carried, and he was mostly dead when he arrived. Atlas had found him brilliant, lifted him toward the clouds, and then closed its hand. What was left breathed through ruin and had nothing left but the one language Atlas could not revoke. He was given a bench, parts, and silence. The first thing built at that bench was the machine that let him keep breathing. Mantle's underworld would spend years repeating rumors about the impossible work that came out of that district, never knowing the first piece of it was a pair of lungs.

RECORD 03 ·· CURRENT ·· [ONGOING]

The tower opened again without ever announcing it. Work began moving in and out through hands that said little and a door that no longer had a sign. It came back changed, better than any licensed shop in the lower city could manage, and changed in quieter ways its owners could never quite name. The man who did it took a dead name's place the way the tower took the winter: completely, and without explanation. The lower city, which knows better than to dig at warm ground, settled on calling the place what it had always called it.

The Spire never went back to being visible. It went back to work. And if some whisper that the engineer answers to something further up the tower, no one has ever climbed high enough to ask.

Everything is priced. Nothing is free, nothing is haggled, and pity has never once adjusted a number.

05

06

What comes through the door stays in the door's memory. The Spire forgets no piece it has touched and no hand that carried it. It has never sold a memory. Don't become the reason it starts.

07

No flags, no causes. The Spire builds for the person in front of it, never for the banner behind them. Bring a war to the tower and you'll carry it back out unfixed.

The Tower, Top to Bottom

The Spire has no ranks. It has floors.

ABOVE ·· The Keeper

Whatever remains of the Gridkeeper, if anything does. No client has ever been higher than the workshop. The Engineer does not say. The Contractor does not answer.

THE WORKSHOP ·· The Engineer

Vael Eclipse. The workshop's hands and its only face below. The work is his; whether the will is his is a question nobody profits from asking.

THE DOOR ·· The Contractor

Moss Aldrich. The door, the ledger, and the bill collector. He meets what comes, carries what's paid for, and retrieves what walks off. A working bounty hunter in his own name; the tower is his steadiest contract, not his leash.

STREET LEVEL ·· The Hands

A scrap runner and a fence the lower city could name but won't. They feed the tower parts and move its smaller errands. Neither has been below the shopfront.

OPEN ACCOUNTS ·· THE TOWER AND ITS NEIGHBORS

No Alliances. Accounts.

ACCOUNT ·· MISTRALThe Viper Pit

Commission work, paid per job, arms-length by mutual disgust for the alternative. The Pit's Handlers occasionally carry containers the checkpoints don't see and comms the Kingdom doesn't hear, and the Pit pays the tower's prices like anyone else. The Viper does not own the Engineer. The Engineer does not ask what's in the containers. Both find this arrangement distasteful and neither has a better one.

ACCOUNT ·· ATLASC.M.D.S.

Two quiet doors that occasionally open onto each other. The tower has been known to do maintenance that appears on no manifest; the company has been known to deliver crates nobody weighed. Each side considers the other a vendor, which is the warmest thing either would say aloud.
